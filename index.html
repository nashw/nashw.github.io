Tarte Tatin

A Tarte Tatin is a French pastry of caramel, apples, and puff pastry. Paul and Prue (aka Tater and Twinkie) would like you to make a beautiful, traditional tart.

The trick with this tart is that it's baked upside-down. Bet you didn't see that coming!

  1. Prepare apples

    Peel, quarter, and core all the apples

  2. Tip

    Preheat oven

    Preheat oven to 400F

  3. Make caramel

    Ingredients Instructions
    • Sugar
    • Butter
    • Water (if necessary)
    • 3/4 cup sugar
    • 4 tbsp butter
    • Water (as needed)

    Melt sugar first, then add butter

    Cook sugar until it turns dark amber, remove from heat, and whisk in butter

    Cheater!

    Pour sugar into skillet and place over medium-high heat and cook, without stirring, until sugar is partially liquefied, about 4 minutes. Whisk until all unmelted sugar disappears into the caramel and nudge the heat down to medium low. Cook until the sugar is dark amber, 1 to 2 minutes (about 350F to 370F). Remove from heat, immediately add butter and whisk to melt and combine. This will hold the color where it is.

  4. Add apples

    Cook apples in caramel

    Cheater!

    Add the apples and cook over medium high heat. Cook, gently stirring and turning to ensure even cooking, until apples soften and begin to turn translucent at the edges and are about 3/4 of the way cooked through, about 10 minutes.

  5. Transfer to cake pan

    Arrange apples in pan, then pour caramel over apples

    Cheater!

    Using tongs, transfer apples, rounded side down, one at a time. Arrange them in a concentric circle around the outside, overlapping each apple by about 1/3 and purposely crowding them. Arrange remaining apples in the center of the ring. Pour any extra caramel in the skillet over the apples. Let this cool for 10 minutes.

  6. Add puff pastry

    Roll dough, cut out circle, and place over apples

    Cheater!

    Roll the dough and cut out a circle about one inch larger than the pan (use another pan as a guide). Top apples with the pastry round, tucking the edges in all around with a spatula.

    Cut a vent or two in the center of the puff pastry.

  7. Bake

    Note: Put pan on a baking sheet to avoid any potential mess.

    35-40 minutes, or until the puff pastry is a deep golden brown on top and apples are bubbling around the edges.

  8. Plate the tart

    Allow to cool, then invert onto plate

    Cheater!

    Run a butter knife around the edges to loosen. Let cool on a wire rack for 15-30 minutes. To invert, top with a serving plate and grasp the pan and plate tightly together as a unit (wearing oven mitts if it is still warm) and flip quickly. Remove the pan. If any apples stick to the pan, just replace them where they should go on the tart.

  9. Serve

    With whipped cream or ice cream. Yummy!

  10. Final step

    Jk no more steps, enjoy! Congrats, you've won Star Baker!
